# Koum — Onboarding & Paywall

> Long onboarding, hard paywall, free trial.
> **Target:** 90 seconds to the aha moment, ~3 minutes to paywall.

---

## Philosophy

Three jobs, in order:

1. **Deliver the aha moment fast.** The user must *feel* the alarm-and-scan mechanic before they are asked to pay. Not see a screenshot of it — do it.
2. **Build commitment through disclosure.** Every question the user answers raises the value of what follows. This is why long onboarding beats short onboarding on conversion: sunk effort plus personalization.
3. **Arrive at the paywall as the obvious next step**, not an interruption.

**Two rules that override everything:**

**Never break tone to sell.** This audience recognizes manipulation instantly and reacts badly to being marketed at within something they consider sacred. No countdown timers, no fake scarcity, no "97% off!!" No manufactured urgency of any kind. Calm, plain, confident.

**Never proselytize.** The user is already Christian. Koum never explains why Scripture matters, never uses conversion language, never adopts a preachy register. It assumes the conviction and supplies only the mechanism. Getting this wrong is the fastest way to feel fake.

```
11 Where do you start?  ← input
12 Alarm permission
13 Your morning, built

15 ▶ PAYWALL
```

**Screens 1–7 are the pitch. 8–13 are configuration. 14–15 are the ask.**

The demo sits at screen 7 — after enough context that it means something, before the user gets bored. Do not move it later.

Black. Type appears line by line, slowly.


## Screen 7 — Live alarm demo ⭐

**The most important screen in the app.** Everything before it is setup; everything after it is bought.

```
        Let's try it.

        Your alarm is about
        to go off.

        Have a Bible nearby —
        or don't, you can type
        it instead.

        [ I'm ready ]
```

Tap → 2 second pause → **the alarm actually fires.** Full volume, full screen, real sound.

```
        ┌─────────────────────┐
        │                     │
        │       6:30          │
        │                     │
        │   PSALM 143:8       │
        │                     │
        │  "Let me hear in    │
        │   the morning of    │
        │   your steadfast    │
        │   love, for in you  │
        │   I trust."         │
        │                     │
        │  [ 📷 Scan ]        │
        │  [ 🎙 Say it ]      │
        │  [ ⌨️ Type it ]      │
        │                     │
        └─────────────────────┘
```

User completes it. Real verification, real pipeline, no simulation. The alarm stops.

```
        ✓

        That's it.

        That's every morning.



        [ Continue ]
```

**Implementation notes:**
- Use a genuinely short, easy verse for the demo. Psalm 143:8 or Lamentations 3:23.
- All three modes must work here — this is the real pipeline, not a mock
- Volume ramps but caps below full alarm volume; they are holding the phone
- If verification fails twice, auto-pass with *"Close enough — it's easier in the morning with the page flat."* **Never** let the demo fail. A failed demo is a lost customer.
- The checkmark and the silence are the product. Hold that screen for a beat before advancing.


**Design rules:**
- Yearly pre-selected, weekly present but visually quieter
- Show the monthly equivalent on yearly — it reframes $29.99 as small
- **No countdown timer. No fake discount. No "limited time."** This audience punishes that.
- Close button visible after 3 seconds, small, top-left — App Store requirement and hiding it invites rejection
- **"No charge for 7 days"** stated plainly; trial anxiety is the main conversion blocker
- Privacy line at the bottom: *"Your prayers and journal stay on your phone."*

**Headline options to A/B:**
- "Start tomorrow morning." *(default — concrete, immediate)*
- "Tomorrow morning starts tonight."
- "Wake up with purpose."

### On hard paywall

You asked for a hard paywall. It is the right call for conversion, but be aware of the specific risk here: **the two strongest competitors are both free-to-download with premium upgrades.** A user comparing options sees Koum asking for money before they can set a single alarm, and Bible Alarm not asking at all.

This is defensible because of the demo — the user has already experienced the mechanic before the ask, which most freemium competitors cannot claim. But watch install-to-trial rate closely in week one. If it is under ~15%, consider a softer gate: let users set one alarm free, paywall the devotional, prayer log, journal, and streaks. That is still a hard product boundary; it just moves the wall past the first morning.

```
        ─────────────────────

        We'll remind you on
        day 5, before the trial ends.

```

The Bible instruction is a real behavioural nudge — environment design beats willpower, and it makes tomorrow's success more likely, which makes conversion more likely.

The day-5 reminder promise is honest, reduces cancel-anxiety, and is worth more in trust than it costs in conversions.

## Trial mechanics

**7 days, card required (Apple standard).**

| Day | Touchpoint |
|---|---|
| 1 | First morning. Push at alarm time. |
| 3 | "Three mornings. That's the hard part done." |
| 5 | **Trial ending reminder** — as promised, honestly |
| 7 | Converts |

**Day 5 message:**
> Your trial ends in two days. You've had 5 mornings with God. If you want to keep going, nothing to do — it renews on Thursday. If not, cancel in Settings, no hard feelings.

That last clause costs a small number of conversions and buys real goodwill in a market where word of mouth travels through churches and group chats.

---

## Win-back

**Cancelled during trial:**
Day 3 after: "The alarm is still there when you want it." Offer 50% off yearly, once, no timer.

**Lapsed subscriber:**
Show their streak record and total mornings completed. *"You had 47 mornings. Want them back?"* Loss of accumulated history is the strongest honest re-conversion lever, and it requires no discount at all.

## Metrics

| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| Onboarding completion (1 → 15) | > 60% |
| Demo completion (screen 7) | > 85% |
| Install → trial start | > 20% (watch closely given free competitors) |
| Trial → paid | > 35% |
| D1 alarm completion | > 70% |
| D7 retention | > 40% |
| D30 retention | > 25% |

**The single most predictive number: did they complete their first real morning?** Users who verify on day 1 convert at multiples of those who do not. Every push notification, every piece of copy on screen 16, exists to make the first morning happen.
